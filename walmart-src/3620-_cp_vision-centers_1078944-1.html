FAQ

How do I order prescription eyewear online?

You can browse for prescription glasses online at Walmart & try your favorite pairs on virtually to see how they look on you. Next, you’ll customize your lenses to your needs & upload your prescription. Then you’ll measure your pupillary distance with our digital tool for a good fit. From there, just add your glasses to cart & check out.

Can I try glasses on virtually?

Yes. When browsing for frames, use the 'Virtual preview' filter to them on virtually. Open on the item that you'd like to try on in real time, then select "Try them on" using your device's camera. If you like the frames, you can select your lenses & proceed with ordering your new glasses.

Where can I find my frame’s size?

To find your size, you can use the measurements from your current glasses. Just look inside the arm of your glasses & you'll see the measurements engraved there. When you're shopping at Walmart, you can check the size guide for frame measurements. Compare those measurements to your current size to make sure you find glasses that fit you well.

Can I order contact lenses?

Yes, you can order your contact lenses from WalmartContacts.com.

Do I need a prescription from my doctor to order?

To buy glasses, you must upload a picture of a current prescription with all the details visible. If your prescription includes your pupillary distance, you can enter it or use our digital measurement tool for a perfect fit.

What if I don’t have a copy of my prescription?

To complete your order, we'll need a picture of your valid prescription. If you don't have it ready, you can add the glasses to your cart & save them for later. Meanwhile, you can ask your doctor for a copy of your prescription or get a new one. Once you have it, you can upload a photo of your prescription from cart & check out.

Can I add a new prescription if I used the wrong one?

If you already paid for your order, you have to cancel it in your order details. Then, you can make a new order with your updated prescription. If you haven't finished checking out yet, you need to remove the item from your cart & add it again with the new prescription.

I don’t know my pupillary distance. How can I get it?

You can use our pupillary distance measurement tool when ordering your new glasses & we’ll take care of it for you. The whole process is online, so you don't need to print any rulers or find your measuring tape.

What payment methods can I use?

We accept credit cards, debit cards & other digital wallet methods as valid payment.

Can I use my insurance to order?

We don't currently accept insurance online, but you can check with your insurance provider about reimbursement for your online order later. If you prefer to use insurance, you can visit our Vision Centers in person to make your purchase.

Can I use my FSA/HSA to order?

We don’t currently accept FSA or HSA cards as a payment method. Please use a credit, debit or gift card to pay for your order with FSA- & HSA-eligible items, then submit your order receipt to your plan administrator for reimbursement.

Are there any special discounts or promotions?

When purchasing prescription glasses, receive 20% off each additional pair of equal or lesser value. If you’re purchasing prescription sunglasses, receive 35% off each additional pair of equal or lesser value.

How can I get my new glasses?

You can order new glasses online & have them shipped directly to your door. You can also order glasses in-store & choose to either ship them to your home or come back in-store & pick them up.

How can I track my order?

You can track your order online at Walmart.com or in the Walmart app by going to ‘My Account’ or via the order details link in your confirmation email. Once your order has shipped, you will receive a tracking link & can see when your package will arrive.

How long will it take to receive my order?

Once you place your order, your new glasses will arrive within 7—9 business days. You can see the estimated arrival date when ordering & under your order details.

What do I do if there’s a problem with my glasses?

Your glasses will be customized to your prescription & measurements. Some final fitting adjustments may be required for your best vision, but we make it simple. Just visit any Walmart Vision Center for free adjustments & cleanings.

What if I want to return my glasses?

Your new glasses are covered under our 60-day customer satisfaction guarantee, so you can return them any time before then. You can start the process online through your order details. At this time, online purchases are only eligible for mail-back returns.

What do I do if my order was canceled due to an issue?

If your order was canceled, you will receive an email with more details to guide you on what to do next. In some cases, there may be an issue with your prescription & we may need a new copy from you. In other situations, your frame & lenses may not be compatible & you may need to select new options to be able to make your glasses.

How do I get my glasses to fit correctly?

Your new glasses may need some final adjustments for your best vision, especially for bifocal & progressive lens wearers. Visit your local Vision Center & our friendly opticians will be happy to help.

How do I check the distance vision for my glasses?

Look up & look into the distance. You should be able to clearly see with your distance vision in a natural position. If you have a progressive lens & you look from the side of the lens, you might notice the view is less sharp. That’s okay. The lens is designed that way—just point your nose at what you want to see.

How do I check the near vision for my glasses?

If you’re in a progressive or bifocal lens, slowly drop your eyes & raise your chin until the view becomes clear on something close by. If you’re wearing a progressive lens, you’ll notice that there’s a sweet spot in the lens at every distance. For new bifocal & progressive wearers, it may take a week or so to get used to your new glasses.

How do I clean my glasses?

For daily cleaning, use a wet wipe or microfiber cloth & glasses cleaner. For a deep clean, rinse the frame & lenses in warm water. Apply soap & wash the frame & lenses with a cloth or your finger. Rinse again & dry with a lint-free cloth. Remove any remaining streaks with a microfiber cloth.

How do I take care of my glasses?

Store your glasses in a case whenever you aren’t wearing them & never lay them down on a table. Avoid high temperatures as heat exposure can damage the frame & lenses. Be especially careful & don’t leave your glasses in a hot car.

Tips for Eyeglass Shopping

Flatter your face

  • Best for oval faces: All shapes. Because oval-shaped faces have balanced features and gentle curves, they can wear nearly any frame shape. To add a little pizzazz, try square and rectangular frames, which contrast with your facial structure. Avoid frames that are overly large or too small to maintain the natural balance of your features.
  • Best for round faces: Square, rectangular and angular. Angular shapes play off of your fuller cheeks and rounded chin. Other shapes to try include those that upsweep at the corners to emphasize your eyes.
  • Best for square faces: Oval and round frames. Your facial structure is characterized by straight lines, including a square chin and a broad forehead. Choose frames with soft, rounded edges to balance the lines of your face, while allowing your features to shine.
  • Best for a heart shaped face: Varies. There's no set rule. Look for frames that balance the varying widths of your facial features. For example, bottom-heavy frames balance the narrowness of the lower half of your face, while oval shapes can soften a more pointed chin.

Frame materials

Choose a frame material that offers the right balance between durability and design. Plastic, or zyl, frames are lightweight and pretty sturdy. They also offer one of the widest color ranges available. If you want something a little more sleek and modern, metal frames such as titanium, beryllium or monel deliver the style you are looking for.

Hinge styles

Don't forget about the importance of hinges. Standard barrel hinges are easy to repair and durable, but they aren't very flexible. If you're hard on your glasses, you may want to opt for flex hinges, which feature a tiny spring that gives the arms of the glasses a little extra movement, making them ideal for active adults and kids.

Shopping Tips for Contact Lenses

Walmart Vision Centers are the one-stop eye care shop you can rely on for everything from prescription glasses and sunglasses to contact lenses. To help you pick the right contacts for you, consider the following factors:

Types of contact lenses

  • Best for people who want easy care: Disposable contact lenses. Use them once, then throw them away. These lenses should be removed at the end of each day. Although they are convenient, they tend to be more expensive than standard daily wear soft contact lenses.
  • Best for people who don't mind a little extra maintenance: Daily wear. These reusable contact lenses require removal and cleaning at the end of every day. They can last up to one year, and they're one of the most economical options. Your optician can help provide recommendations about the types of cleaning solutions available.
  • Best for people who don't want to remove their lenses every day: Extended wear lenses. Extended wear contact lenses are designed to wear continually for one to four weeks. Although they're convenient, sleeping in contacts can increase your chances of developing an eye infection, so be sure to talk to a Walmart Vision Center professional before choosing extended wear contacts for the first time.

Contact lens brands

If you already wear contact lenses, you may want to stick with the brand you're familiar with. But if you're new to contacts or interested in trying something new, you're in luck. Walmart's Vision Center carries all the major brand names that you trust at low prices you can count on.
