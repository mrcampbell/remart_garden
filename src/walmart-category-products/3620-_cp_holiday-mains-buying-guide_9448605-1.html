Turkey Serving & Cooking Time Guide

How much turkey will I need for Thanksgiving dinner?

If you’re serving a whole turkey, the general rule of thumb is about 1½ pounds of turkey per person. If you’re serving boneless turkey breast, plan for about ½ pound per person.

Here’s a handy size guide for buying a whole turkey:

- One 11- to 14-pound turkey feeds about 7 to 9 people

- One 15- to 18-pound turkey feeds about 10 to 12 people

- One 18- to 24-pound turkey feeds about 12 to 14 people

How long does it take to defrost a frozen turkey?

The safest way to thaw a frozen turkey is in the refrigerator. Keep the turkey in its original packaging and allow to thaw in the fridge for 24 hours for every 4 to 5 pounds of turkey. That means a 12-pound turkey will take about 3 days to thaw in the fridge, and a 20-pound turkey will take about 5 days to thaw in the fridge.

How long does it take to roast a whole turkey?

Cooking times vary depending on the temperature of your oven, the size of the turkey, and whether it is stuffed. You’ll know your turkey is fully cooked when a meat thermometer reaches 180°F in the thickest part of the thigh, 170°F in the turkey breast, and 165°F in the stuffing (if using).

Here’s a handy guide to roasting a whole, unstuffed turkey at 325°F in a regular oven:

- 7 to 10 pounds takes about 2½ to 3 hours

- 10 to 18 pounds takes about 3 to 3½ hours

- 18 to 22 pounds takes about 3½ to 4 hours

- 22 to 24 pounds takes about 4 to 4½ hours
