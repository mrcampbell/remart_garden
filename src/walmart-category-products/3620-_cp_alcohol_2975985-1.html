What's the difference between regular and light beer?

Light beers have fewer calories than regular beers, and they may also be lower in alcohol. Brewers may dilute regular beer with water, or they might use a longer mashing process to break down more of the carbohydrates into simple sugars. Fewer carbs mean fewer calories. There aren't industry standards for what makes a light beer. A beer is light if it has fewer calories than the regular version.

What are some sparkling wine and food pairings?

Champagne or sparkling wine pairs well with steak due to the richness of the meat and the flavors of the sparkling wine. Champagne also pairs with creamy soups due to the subtle flavors of the soup and herbs such as parsley and thyme. Try champagne with oysters too. The briny, rich flavors of the oysters and the texture of the champagne blend well together.

What liquors and mixers should I stock in my home bar for making cocktails?

The five basic liquors to stock include vodka, gin, tequila, rum, and whiskey. Vodka is most prevalent in mixed cocktails. Stocking a variety of mixers allows you to make different cocktails. Keep juices such as orange, cranberry, lemon, lime, and tomato on hand. Club soda, simple syrup, bitters, and sour mix are also basic cocktail ingredients.
