Types of Coffees FAQ's

What is a cold brew?

Cold brew coffee is made by slowly steeping coarsely ground coffee beans in room temperature water for at least six hours (it can be as much as up to 24, but it depends on how strong you want your cold brew to be, many choose to do it overnight). When the beans are steeped like this, it creates a strong coffee concentrate that just needs to be mixed with water & milk to taste. As the grounds aren't exposed to hot temperatures, cold brew coffee has a more flavorful, sweeter & less bitter/acidic taste. In a recent study, cold brew coffee was found to be between 50-60% less acidic than the average cup of coffee. It's a common misbelief that cold brew has to be cold, in actual fact it can be served piping hot too! If you want it cold, simply add cold water, ice & milk (to taste); for hot you just need to boil water or add hot milk (some will even pop it in the microwave if they're in a rush).

What is espresso?

Espresso is delicious, concentrated form of coffee, served in shots & is often the coffee base of many other beverages, such as cappuccino, latte, Americano & macchiato. Espresso is intense & bold, but nonetheless delicious & the espresso is usually served in small demitasse-style cups for this reason.

What is an Americano?

Americano is just hot water & espresso. It'll either be served 1/2 & 1/2 or 1/3 espresso to 2/3 water, depending on how you've chosen to brew it. There is dispute about whether the espresso or the water should be added first, but in the case of the Americano, espresso should always be added first as the crema mixes in an creates a more mellow, even taste. Be some people may add it depending on their personal preference. Most other espresso-based coffee drinks include milk, such as the cappuccino or latte, the Americano stands out amongst them.

What is an Irish Coffee?

Irish coffee has four main ingredients: coffee, Irish whiskey, sugar & cream. But there are many variations of this classic coffee drink. When making an Irish coffee, it's important to ensure the coffee is strong & the whiskey is Irish, as it's simply not an Irish coffee if you use Scotch whiskey. The coffee drink is drunk through its creamy head. To ensure the cream floats on top of the coffee & doesn't sink, you can whip or blend the cream before layering it on top of the coffee with a spoon.

What does ethical sourcing mean for coffee beans?

Ethical sourcing involves the use of farming practices that are environmentally sustainable. These methods of farming won't affect the land in adverse ways. Ethical sourcing also means that the coffee farmers are paid fairly for their work.

What's the best way to store coffee?

Store both ground and whole bean coffee at room temperature, out of direct sunlight, and in an airtight, opaque container. Freezing may create moisture in the container, which can affect the coffee taste. If you buy whole beans in bulk, it's fine to store them in the freezer for up to one month as long as you don't take them in and out of the freezer, which can allow partial thawing. Store coffee pods in a cool dry place also.

Should I wait and grind coffee beans right before I make coffee?

Grinding the beans immediately before you make coffee will result in the most flavor, because the ground coffee won't have a chance to oxidize. If you do need to grind the beans ahead of time, store the coffee in a sealed container in a cool and dark spot.
