About Virtual Reality - Walmart.com

Should I get a wired or wireless VR headset?

Both types of headsets have their advantages, so it depends on what works best for your setup and how you plan to use your headset. Wireless VR headsets give you more freedom of movement. Depending on the headset you purchase, you may have unlimited freedom or you might have a radius of 25 to 100 meters from your PC or smartphone. When you're moving around, you don't have to worry about stepping over—or tripping over—cords, but you may have to rely on weaker processing speed, leading to poorer image quality. A wired VR headset typically renders the best visual experience, but you will need to deal with the limitations of being tethered to a PC.

Do you need a game for a VR headset?

You'll generally need to buy games with your VR headset. Some systems have built-in games offered for free or purchase. When you're new to VR, it's best to start with short, 15-minute games as it takes most people time to get used to the experience before they can go longer.

What is a standalone VR headset?

A standalone VR headset does not require a PC or smartphone to function. It includes everything you need to enjoy the VR experience, including a battery, a built-in screen processor, and controllers. This is a great choice if you want to use your VR headset on the go and don't want to rely on a PC or smartphone.
