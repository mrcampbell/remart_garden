About Bikes - Walmart.com

Adult bikes

There are many ways to get around, but biking is undoubtedly the best way to get in shape while you get where you need to be. Want to start biking to work? Or maybe just take a leisurely ride on the weekends? Maybe you're a more experienced rider and you need something that can handle rough terrain. We'll help you find what you need, from tops brands like Schwinn, Mongoose, and Huffy.

Types of bikes

  • A cruiser bike is a fun and simple bike to use. It's perfect for casual riding on a beach, road, or bike path. Its safe design makes it easy to control, and it offers a stable ride for riders of any ability. Cruiser bikes have padded seats, thick tires, and coaster brakes, which are brakes that stop when you push backward on the pedals. Some bikes offer all the amenities of a cruiser bike but also have hand brakes for a smooth, precise stop.
  • Want to start biking to work or school? Look no further than a commuter bike. A commuter bike is lightweight yet agile and is safe in rainy conditions. A front commuting light helps make traveling home from work in the dark safe and easy. A hybrid bike is another great option for commuting to work or even for long rides on a trail. The comfortable seats of a hybrid make this a bike you'll want to ride every day.
  • Mountain bikes (MTBs) are versatile and come with a variety of features. This makes purchasing one within your budget a breeze. Whether you want a model with 18 speeds or 24 speeds, you can find the exact mountain bike (mtb) to suit your lifestyle. Look for bikes with front and rear mechanical disc brakes to ensure a complete stop no matter the weather. Tool-free adjustable seats allow you to pack light for your next trip.

Finding the right size bike

For the sake of safety and comfort, it's important to find a model that fits you. For example, a women's bike will have a shorter stack height and shorter reach lengths. Make sure you're getting the correct size bike by checking the manufacturer's user manual for information on height and adjustability before purchasing.

  • After you find a bike that fits, you'll more than likely need to adjust the seat. When sitting on the seat, your leg should be almost completely straight when you push the pedal all the way down.
  • Next are the handlebars. You should be able to reach to the handlebars with your elbows bent at a slight 10-degree angle.
